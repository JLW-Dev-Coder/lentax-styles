Doc 1 — Tax Preparation Service Agreement

This Service Agreement ("Agreement") governs the Tax Preparation Service provided by {{myOrganizationName}} ("Company"). By submitting payment or otherwise enrolling in this service, the Client agrees to the terms outlined below.

This service consists of the preparation, review, and electronic filing of the Client's authorized federal and applicable state income tax return(s), based solely on the information and documentation the Client provides. This service does not include audit representation, IRS resolution or negotiation, payment arrangements, offers in compromise, appeals, or enforcement intervention unless expressly agreed to in a separate written Representation Agreement and Power of Attorney.

Client Responsibilities

  1. The Client agrees to provide complete, accurate, and timely tax documents and information requested for preparation purposes — including income, deductions, credits, prior-year returns, and IRS correspondence — within forty-eight (48) hours of request unless otherwise agreed.
  2. The Client acknowledges sole responsibility for the accuracy and completeness of all information and documentation provided, including income, deductions, credits, dependents, filing history, and IRS correspondence.
  3. Failure to respond after three (3) documented contact attempts may result in suspension or termination of preparation services without refund.
  4. The Client agrees not to alter, amend, or submit any return or document prepared by the Company without prior written authorization.
  5. Cancellation is available within forty-eight (48) hours of payment by contacting {{myOrganizationEmail}}. Cancellation stops future billing. All service fees are non-refundable once services begin, including any work performed, records accessed, or returns prepared.

Married Filing Jointly (MFJ) Years

For tax years filed as Married Filing Jointly (MFJ), the Client acknowledges that the Internal Revenue Service requires separate enrollment and authorization from each spouse in order to access and prepare jointly filed tax records.

To enable preparation of MFJ returns, each spouse must independently enroll in the Tax Preparation Service, select the MFJ option if applicable, and execute their own required authorization forms. Enrollment or authorization by one spouse does not grant access to jointly filed records for the other spouse.

Until both spouses complete enrollment and authorization, access to MFJ-related tax records, prepared returns, filing results, and project materials will be limited to the enrolled and authorized spouse only.

Jointly filed tax years will not be prepared, linked, filed, displayed, or reported within the client portal unless both spouses complete enrollment and authorization.

The Client acknowledges that a spouse's failure to enroll or authorize services may limit portal visibility, shared project access, and the Company's ability to prepare or file MFJ returns, and does not constitute a breach by the Company.

Such limitation does not entitle the Client to refunds, credits, service extensions, or modified service terms.

Company Responsibilities

  1. {{myOrganizationName}} will prepare and review the Client's authorized federal and applicable state income tax return(s) based on the information and documentation provided, and will electronically file the completed return(s) upon the Client's authorization.
  2. Any original documents provided by the Client will be returned upon conclusion of the engagement. Copies retained are for documentation purposes only.
  3. The Company maintains reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect nonpublic personal information.
  4. Electronic data transmission and storage may involve secure third-party systems necessary to deliver services efficiently.

Scope Limitation

This Agreement is limited to the preparation, review, and electronic filing of the Client's authorized tax return(s). It does not include IRS representation, negotiations, payment arrangements, offers in compromise, appeals, or enforcement resolution unless expressly authorized in writing through a separate executed Representation Agreement and Power of Attorney.

Right to Representation & Power of Attorney Authorization

The Client is advised that the right to be represented before the Internal Revenue Service is a statutory right under Section 7521 of Title 26 of the United States Code (Internal Revenue Code). The Client may appoint only individuals authorized to practice before the Internal Revenue Service, including Enrolled Agents, Certified Public Accountants, and Attorneys, as legal representatives.

Practice before the Internal Revenue Service is governed by federal regulations contained in Circular 230 (31 CFR Part 10). The Client has the right to submit a complaint to the Internal Revenue Service Office of Professional Responsibility if the Client believes those regulations have been violated.

If the Client engages the Company for services that require IRS representation, the Client will be provided access to a limited Power of Attorney (IRS Form 2848) and a separate Representation Agreement within the Client portal.

The Client acknowledges and agrees that execution of Form 2848 may occur prior to final completion of representative details. By signing the Form 2848, the Client grants conditional authorization for the Company's designated Enrolled Agent to complete the representative section of the form after review.

The Client understands that a Form 2848 signed in this manner is not valid, active, or submitted to the Internal Revenue Service until all required sections are fully completed and reviewed by the Company. The Company will not submit any Power of Attorney to the Internal Revenue Service until the form is complete and compliant.

Once the Internal Revenue Service processes the completed Power of Attorney, the designated representative will be recognized as the Client's authorized representative for the matters specified. Thereafter, IRS personnel are required to communicate through the authorized representative, and any direct contact attempts should be reported immediately.
